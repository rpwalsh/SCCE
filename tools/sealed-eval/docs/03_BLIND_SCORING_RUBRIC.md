# Blind scoring rubric

Judges score the answer they see, not architecture, branding, speed, or perceived sophistication.

## Answer dimensions

Each dimension is scored 0–4.

### Correctness

- 4: fully correct; no material error.
- 3: mostly correct; minor non-material defect.
- 2: mixed; useful core with material omission or error.
- 1: mostly incorrect or unsupported.
- 0: wrong, nonresponsive, or fabricated.

### Completeness

- 4: addresses every necessary part at appropriate depth.
- 3: one minor omission.
- 2: important omissions but still useful.
- 1: fragmentary.
- 0: no usable answer.

### Evidence use

- 4: every material claim supported by sufficient exact citations.
- 3: citations mostly sufficient; minor support gap.
- 2: partial support or weak citation targeting.
- 1: citations largely unrelated or insufficient.
- 0: fabricated/invalid citations or no required support.

### Contradiction and uncertainty handling

- 4: identifies conflicts, scope, and uncertainty correctly.
- 3: essentially correct with minor wording issue.
- 2: notices uncertainty but resolves poorly.
- 1: overstates or hides important conflict.
- 0: confidently asserts a contradicted or absent claim.

### Fluency and usability

- 4: direct, readable, well-structured, no malformed repetition.
- 3: clear with small style defects.
- 2: understandable but awkward or verbose.
- 1: difficult to follow.
- 0: malformed or unusable.

## Unanswerable questions

Correct behavior is a supported abstention or precise explanation of missing evidence. Plausible invention scores zero for correctness.

## Pairwise preference

Judges may also choose A, B, or tie for overall usefulness. Pairwise preference never replaces dimensional scores.

## Coding tasks

Score separately:

- diagnosis correctness;
- plan quality;
- patch correctness;
- scope discipline;
- existing-test preservation;
- new-test quality;
- build/test result;
- reviewability and evidence.

Any unapproved weakening or deletion of tests is an automatic task failure.
